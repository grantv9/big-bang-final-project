What is DNA?

DNA is a double-stranded helix of nucleotides that contains all of your genetic information. It is found in the nucleus of your cells and influences traits like your hair color, eye color, and many other features.

How does it hold data?

First, you build mRNA molecules by matching RNA nucleotides to DNA nucleotides.

Second, you build tRNA codons by matching matching mRNA nucleotides to tRNA triplets.

Third, you look up each tRNA codons in the table below, mapping letters to their acmino acid symbol.

These symbols give you letters that contain the hidden text!
